Our Mission at AgriCRBN

As we look ahead to addressing some of the nation’s most pressing challenges, one theme emerges as a unifying solution: soil health. Whether we’re discussing agriculture, environmental restoration, or energy policy; soil health is the foundation for creating a healthier planet, healthier people, and a more resilient economy. Healthy soils grow healthy food. They also filter our water and purify our air. Removing harmful chemicals and pesticides from farming practices protects our health and creates safer communities. But today, our soils are under threat. Decades of mono-cropping, excessive pesticide use, and synthetic fertilizers have degraded our lands, putting the future of farming - and the health of our planet - at risk. Fossil fuel production is often viewed as being at odds with some initiatives that aim to address environmental and public health. But improving soil health can serve as the critical bridge. Healthy soils have the capacity to sequester massive amounts of carbon, offsetting emissions and mitigating climate change. Furthermore, the carbon stored in the soil fuels microbial life enhances productivity, turning a challenge into an opportunity. We are excited about the possibilities of collaborating with leaders who share our vision of harnessing innovation and natural solutions to address the challenges of our time. Together we can position the United States as a global leader in sustainable agriculture, carbon sequestration, and environmental stewardship. The time to act is now! Let’s turn our challenges into opportunities. Join us in this movement to restore our soils, empower our farmers and secure a sustainable future for generations to come. Let’s make a difference - one acre at a time.

Healthy Soil

Healthy soil is the foundation of life. It’s where clean air, clean water, and nutritious food production begin. Biochar enhances soil porosity, boosts water retention, and increases nutrient availability. When combined with organic liquid fertilizers, it accelerates soil regeneration, achieving in just a few years what would naturally take centuries. Biochar doesn’t just lock carbon into the soil for centuries - it also prevents carbon dioxide off-gassing from deadfall and improves soil health. By sequestering carbon in the soil, biochar addresses legacy carbon emissions while reducing the dependency on synthetic fertilizers. Cover crops and regenerative practices further increase yields, capturing even more carbon in the process. Previous initiatives to reduce fuel loads in our forests to prevent wildfires was an important step toward better land management. Moving forward, this initiative can play a key role in advancing another critical goal: the production of biochar. By converting forest residues into biochar, we not only reduce the risk of devastating wildfires but also create a powerful tool for rebuilding soil health and sequestering carbon.

How We Do It

Every year, beetle-killed trees leave behind more than 100 acres of deadfall in the U.S. alone. This debris fuels catastrophic wildfires and releases massive amounts of carbon into the atmosphere. What if this problem could become part of the solutions? Our process begins by taking forest debris and heating it in an oxygen-depleted kiln at a temperature up to one thousand degrees Fahrenheit. The result? A carbon-rich material called biochar, with an immense surface area and remarkable properties. At AgriCRBN, biochar is a cornerstone of our fertilizer and soil amendment products. Biochar enhances soil structure, increases nutrient retention, and improves water-holding capacity. Most importantly, it acts as a long-term carbon sink, helping to draw down atmospheric carbon and complete the natural carbon cycle. Our approach to soil health doesn’t just address climate and environmental concerns. It also supports agricultural resilience, food security, and public health. By integrating practices like regenerative agriculture, biochar production, and holistic forest management, we can chart a path toward a more sustainable and prosperous future.

Rural economies thrive as biochar production and regenerative farming create new jobs. Communities become more resilient, mitigating the risks of wildfires and droughts while sequestering carbon and reducing greenhouse gas emissions. Let’s help our farmers and growers transition to practices that restore soil, improve food security, and mitigate climate change - all while ensuring economic growth.
